About Me

On the professional side, I'm always looking for the next thing to learn and build. This site is a good example — I used AI to help me navigate purchasing a domain, setting up email routing, building and deploying the front end, integrating third-party tools, and everything in between. Not because I couldn't eventually figure it out alone, but because I wanted to see how far I could get by working smarter. I'm currently renewing my AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner certification and working toward my Okta Administrator certification — both a natural extension of the work I do every day.

I've spent most of my career at the intersection of people and technology — figuring out what's broken, building what's missing, and making things easier for the folks around me. What keeps me hooked on tech is simple: it never stops moving. For someone with ADHD, that's less of a challenge and more of a feature.

My path here wasn't exactly linear. I served five years as a Military Policeman in the Marine Corps — stationed in Okinawa, Japan, where I got to travel across the Pacific before deploying to Afghanistan. Back stateside at Camp Pendleton, I trained Marines on what I'd learned downrange, then transitioned to the Provost Marshal's Office enforcing Federal and State law alongside federal officers before I got out. The Corps built a foundation that shows up in everything I do.

While serving I fell in love with fitness — first as a runner, then bodybuilding, powerlifting, and eventually strongman. What hooked me was being able to see progress in real time: grinding through 6 sets of 3 at 315 with straps, then one day repping the same weight for 10 without them. Double Achilles tendonitis had other plans, so the focus has shifted to staying strong and moving well — a lot more mobility work and cardio than I'd like to admit.

Outside of work I'm a family man through and through. My blended family of six keeps life full — two older boys, two younger girls, and a schedule that revolves around sports sidelines and exploring the Pacific Northwest. I'm originally from Bird Creek, Alaska, and after years of moving around — Washington, Alaska, Nevada, the Marines — I landed back in Washington for family, and everything else has fallen into place. I recently picked up a compound bow, and the plan is to document the whole journey from beginner to first harvest. I was a five-time expert rifleman in the Marines, so rifle hunting never felt like the right challenge — I'd rather earn it the hard way, up close.

If I seem like someone who overshares a little — that's accurate, and I've made peace with it.
